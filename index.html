Kaggle Project: Mobility Report Analysis (Baden-Württemberg, Germany)

This project analyzed Google Mobility data from Baden-Württemberg (BW), Germany, in 2020 to uncover the relationship between human mobility patterns and consumer purchasing behavior during COVID-19. Key Findings Summary

This analysis presents deviations from the baseline mobility across various categories in the **Baden-Württemberg (BW)** region of Germany during 2020. The values represent percentage changes from pre-COVID baseline levels, helping to understand behavioral trends over time.

Monthly Trends:
  • Grocery & Pharmacy: Mobility remained relatively stable throughout the year, with moderate deviations (Min: -11.17, Max: 5.03).
  • Parks: Consistently above baseline, peaking in August (137.2%), indicating high public engagement.
  • Residential Areas: Activity levels remained largely unchanged throughout the year (Max: 13.43 in April, Min: 1.3 in September).
  • Retail & Recreation: Consistently below baseline, reflecting cautious consumer behavior, with a plateau during July, August, and September (Min: -51.15, Max: -0.18).
  • Transit Stations: Always below baseline, indicating reduced reliance on public transport, with a downward trend toward December (Min: -48.07 in April, Max: -0.08 in February).
Day-wise Trends (Average Over the Year):
  • Grocery & Pharmacy: Clear preference for shopping at the start and end of each month (Max: 9.88 on 2nd day, Min: -16.28 on 1st day).
  • Parks: Remained the most preferred category with consistently positive deviations, peaking around every 10 days (1st, 11th, 21st).
  • Residential Areas: Showed negligible fluctuation, indicating a uniform pattern throughout the year.
  • Retail & Recreation: Consistently below baseline, but showed minor peaks during the start, middle, and end phases of each month.
  • Transit Stations: Activity remained consistently negative, with slight peaks around the middle of the month.

Tools Used

  • BigQuery: Used to query, filter, and clean the raw Google Mobility data for the BW region (2020).
  • Python (Kaggle Notebooks): Assisted in exploring file structures, managing extractions, and structuring observations for documentation.
  • Tableau: Visualized month-wise and day-wise mobility trends through interactive dashboards for pattern discovery.
  • Kaggle: Used as a collaborative environment to document, analyze, and present findings in a reproducible format.
